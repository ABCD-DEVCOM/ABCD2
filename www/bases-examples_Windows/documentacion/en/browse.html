Access permissions in ABCD 

The ABCD Operators or users are the people who have access to the different functions and databases as defined in the system. There are 8 types of Operators:

System Administrators

They have access to all the modules, functions and databases defined under ABCD. System Administrators are also in charge of defining new users and assign access permissions

Database Administrators

They can:

  • Create, modify and delete database structures
  • Perform all the administrative tasks related to the databases that are under their control (modify structure, fst's, configuration of statistics, unblock databases and records, block databases to stop data entry, export and import records)
  • Perform global changes on the databases that are under their control
  • Assign Operators to the databases that are under their control

Database Operators

They can:

  • Create and edit records in the databases that are under their control
  • Generate reports and other services in the databases that are under their control

Loans Administrators

They can:

  • Define and Modify lending policies
  • Perform all the functions related to the lending system
  • Manage the databases of the lending system: transactions, reservations, suspensions and fines
  • Delete reservations, fines and suspensions

Loans Operators

They can:

  • Perform all the transactions related to the lending service
  • Obtain reports and products

Create New Operator

Click on New User to create a new ABCD operator. A worksheet is presented with the required fields. Fill in the necessary data and click:

  • Save record, or
  • Cancel

depending on the required action. 

[top]

Edit Operator

Click the editing icon associated with the operator you wish to modify. A worksheet is presented with the required fields. Edit the necessary data and click save or cancel depending on the required action.

[top]

Eliminate Operator

Click the eliminate icon associated with the operator you wish to delete.
